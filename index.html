I am commited to building artificial intelligence to better understand the effect of interventions, such as the application of a medical therapy for example, to ensure the safe deployment of these algorithms in the world. My current research efforts focus on essential prerequisites of such AI:

Causality, Fairness and prediction models for high-dimensional and time series data

I am currently a PhD student in Machine Learning at the University of Cambridge and the Alan Turing Institute. Before joining my PhD program I graduated from a MSc in Applied Statistics from the University of Oxford and a BSc in Mathematics from Imperial College London. My CV (as of December 2019) is available here and a list of publications is available below.
